ReZero: Region-wise Zone extraction for Speech Enhancement

Anonymous Authors

Abstract:

Region-wise speech enhancement (R-SE) has attracted increasing attention owing to the relaxed requirement for target speaker information (e.g., clean speaker enrollment or precise speaker direction/location) and the number of speakers. However, existing R-SE models typically make use of fixed or pre-defined angular or distance queries, while lacking the flexibility to dynamically adjust them per needed. Towards more general R-SE with alterable region types and scopes, this work proposes Region-wise Zone extraction (ReZero), a unified R-SE framework, to extract and dereverberate all the speech signals within the query region from the mixture. We further propose a multi-channel extension of the band-split RNN (BSRNN) model to enhance the overall performance. During evaluation, the azimuth, elevation and distance range of the query zone can be dynamically selected to obtain different enhancement results as needed. Experimental results demonstrate the feasibility and potential of the proposed ReZero model for general R-SE.

Demo on synthetic data (3-mic, mono output)


ReZero can be defined in any microphone configurations or applications, and is capable of handling alterable region types and scopes.
Here we consider a typical smart phone based voice communication scenario, where the microphones are located compactly within a smart-phone-size box.
The figure below shows:
    (a) The geometry and spacings of the adopted 3-element microphone array;
    (b) A top view of different query regions performed on the same mixture;
    (c) Angular-window-based zone extraction: all the potential cases of the number of targets (0, 1, or 2) within the zone are covered;
    (d) Closest talk extraction.
It is observed that ReZero can produce different enhancement results when alternating the query region types and scopes to meet different requirements in versatile environments.

Demo on real-recorded data (2-mic, stereo output)


The ReZero model trained on synthetic data is evaluated on real-recorded stereo data. The microphone spacing between two recording sensors is 14cm for the presented demo. The evaluation is also conducted on 2-mic recordings with 10.5cm and 7cm spacings.
The model is causal.
The figure below illustrates the conversation scene in a conference room (about 10 x 6 x 4 m^3). A conference table is placed at the center of the room with the dual-mic put on the table. Two male speakers sit by the table at 90° and 0° relative to the microphone array, respectively. The distance-to-array is about 1.5 meters.
